DREAM Interrupted for UCSB Student

After a run in with local police, UCSB Student Cindy Reyna faces deportation. A petition to save Reyna from deportation is being circulated by members of the UCSB group IDEAS.

 

Nourish International: New Kids on the Block

UCSB’s newest student organization aims to aid to build classrooms and a soccer field for war afflicted children in Nepal. The group is partnering with Inspire A Child to provide energy-creating soccer balls for families without electricity.

 

 

Search for the Santa Catalina Burglar

Campus police are looking for a young man suspected of burglarizing the Santa Catalina Residence Halls. Items taken include four laptop computers, an iPad and a laundry basket.

 

Apple About to Take Over The World

The Cupertino-based company, Apple, is now valued at $418 billion, due to upward movement on Wall Street, making it the world’s most valuable company in terms of market value.

 

 

Port Huron: A Dream 50 Years In The Making

Fifty years ago, students in Port Huron, Michigan decided to formally address pressing civil rights issues by creating a manifesto of radical thinking introduced at a conference of the Students for a Democratic Society; this became known as the Port Huron Statement. This statement will be revisited by the creators at UCSB this week.
